Kilinochchi Central coloage

The first school of Kilinochchi district which was established in 1927 by sir Pon. Ramanathan in the heart of the town, where the Kilinochchi Maha Vidyalayam is operated at present time close to Kakkakadai junction along vilson road surrounded by the source of water and green land, the only aim was to give the proper education to the children of workers who had been deploying at construction work of Iranaimadu tank. The Paranthan chemical factory was started by the great effort of Mr. G. G. Ponnambalam. So that the kids of the employers also began to get to this school, the population of students were suddenly increased, on the other hand the rooms for pupils was lack. In 1960 the people settled at Thirunagar then the number of students was increased from 250 to approximately 1000 in 1965. The principal Late. Mr. M. Shanmuganathan thought to provide a suitable environment of education, so he tried to get a new land for his good purpose. During that period of time parliamentarian Mr.V. Ananthashankari, Parents Teachers Association and as well as School Development Board also gave their fullest support. So the effort of them was fruit full. According to the instruction of government agent of Jaffna, 50 acres of land was handed over to the school in 1947, though the land was very thick jungle. However the principal, the people of neighboring villagers and Rural Development Society.. Who made a Shramadana for the preparation of 4 acres of land. Member of Parliament Mr.V.Ananthashankari and education department provided financial support to construct a new building of school. In 21.09.1977 the Kilinochchi Maha Vidyalayam began its journey so the day could be a milestone for the blossom of present Kilinochchi Central College, where there classes from grade 7 upto A/L function. The grades from 1 to 6 functioned at earliest location. In 1982 the new classes were started at newly constructed building complex. In addition pupils took very keen interest not only in studies but also extra-curricular activities. One Mast.K.Gowreesvaran student of our school was selected to medical faculty. In 1982 Kilinochchi cluster school system was introduced Mr. R. Sinnathamby was promoted as the cluster principal, and then Mr. S. Gunasingarasa was appointed as the principal to this school. Most of the students are from very poor family like lab our parents and farmers. Due to the war the school was badly damaged in the years of 2007 and 2008. In 2010, this school was functioning as transist camp of internally displaced persons. So, temporarily this school had to operate under the roof of NAITA just along the A9 main road at the same year the school had been included under the ‘Isuru School System’, from then primary division and secondary section was started to function separately. Finally in 2011 the building of school was renovated and started to continue its teaching learning process at own premises.
